How do I stake Nexus?

Staking is an energy efficient form of mining that provides security to the Nexus blockchain. NXS can only be staked inside the official Nexus Desktop Wallet with staking enabled, and with continuous Internet connection (24 hours a day, 7 days a week).
In return, you are rewarded NXS at a stake rate of 0.5% to 3.0% per year on your staked holdings. To determine your % stake rate, Nexus has made a time-weighted reputation mechanism called 'trust' that records the consistent time that a node has contributed to processing transactions.
Currently, it takes one year of consistent staking to build up enough Trust to earn a 3.00% rate of return.
To learn more about staking, please visit the Staking page on our Wiki.

Is there a minimum amount of NXS required to stake?

It is possible to stake with any amount of NXS and earn stake rewards at a rate of 0.5%. However, to grow your stake rate and reach the maximum of 3.0%, your node must find a Proof of Stake block at least every 72 hours.
As of April 2023 it takes around 30,000 NXS to do this.

What is a Genesis transaction?

Genesis is the process by which a wallet creates a trust key to use for staking. This is the first step for staking a new wallet. When it creates a new trust key, the Genesis transaction will transfer your wallet balance to that key for staking. This has no impact on your wallet balance.
After the trust key is created, the wallet will no longer stake Genesis, and will instead stake Trust transactions.
Balance must have a minimum coin age of 3 days (72 hours) before the wallet will begin attempting to create a Genesis transaction.
The Genesis transaction takes longer to generate than subsequent Trust transactions. For lower balances, it may stake for multiple weeks before you receive this.

What are stake rate, trust weight, block weight, and stake weight?

These items affect the size and frequency of staking rewards after you receive your initial Genesis transaction. You can view the definitions of these terms in the Glossary.

What affects block production in staking?

For new wallets staking Genesis, total balance and trust weight calculated from coin age. For wallets after Genesis, total balance, block weight, and trust weight calculated from trust score. You can view the definitions of these terms in the Glossary.

What happens when my Block Weight reaches 100%?

Trust weight and stake rate will begin to decay. Without a transaction they will eventually decay back to 0.5%. Generating a new Trust transaction resets block weight, and trust weight/ stake rate continue increasing from their current values. Trust decays at a 3:1 rate to which it was gained. For a new key, it takes 1 day for 3 days of trust to decay. An established key holds onto trust much longer.

Establishing a new trust key is not immediate, by design. The network does not give away trust for free. You may see staking values decay back to the start multiple times before it manages to generate transactions frequently enough to keep going. If it decays back to the start, the wallet will display minimal trust weight of 1.11% with stake rate 0.5% and block weight 100% until it generates a transaction. This is normal. It is not stuck.

What happens if I remove some stake?

When you remove stake, there is an associated cost. If you remove a portion of your stake, it also reduces your Stake Rate. This is to reward those who consistently stake long term and do not frequently reduce their committed Stake amount. If you require frequent access to a portion of your NXS, consider keeping it in another account (such as your Default Account) and not committing it to stake.

I have not received a Trust transaction in a few days, is that normal?

There is no set time period in which it finds a new block. It is not at all unusual to go a period of time without a Trust transaction. They are more frequent if you have a higher balance and can vary somewhat as trust weight and block weight increase. As long as you find at least one Proof of Stake block which generates a Trust transaction within the 72 hour requirement, your trust and stake rate will increase.
If it takes longer than 72 hours, trust and stake rate decay at a rate of 3:1. In other words, you lose 3 days of increase for every 1 day it exceeds 72 hours between blocks.

What is a hard fork, and how does it affect the Nexus Blockchain?

A hard fork is a significant upgrade or change to the blockchain protocol that may result in a split or an incompatibility with previous versions. In this case, the upcoming hard fork will cause the depreciation of legacy UTXO coins, meaning they will no longer be valid or supported after the fork.

What does “depreciation of UTXO coins” mean for my NXS holdings?

Depreciation of UTXO (Unspent Transaction Output) coins means that the legacy coins you hold in the current format will no longer be valid or usable after the hard fork. To continue using your Nexus assets, you must migrate them to the new system before the fork occurs.

How can I tell if my NXS must be migrated before the hard fork?

To determine if your NXS holdings need to be migrated before the hard fork, you can check the following:

Wallet Type: If you're holding your NXS in a much older QT wallet that still requires a Wallet.dat file then migration is required. Users who utilize a Signature Chain (Username, Password, and PIN) to access their wallet are on the Tritium protocol and do not need to migrate.

Checking the Explorer: Navigate to the Nexus Explorer (explorer.nexus.io) and copy/paste your wallet address into the search bar. If your address successfully comes up in the search results, then you do NOT need to migrate. If your address is missing or shows no results, then you must migrate your Legacy NXS.

Wallet Upgrade: If your Nexus Desktop wallet is up to date (Version 3.1.5) or if you are using the Nexus Mobile Wallet, then you do not need to take any actions. If you are running an older version of the Nexus Desktop wallet in “LEGACY MODE” then you must migrate your NXS.

Exchanges: If your NXS holdings are stored on the exchanges TradeOgre or Xeggex, then you do not need to take any actions as the exchange wallets are using the Tritium protocol and are up to date. However, it is recommended that you store your holding on the Nexus Wallets due to exchange operations volatility.

Check with Support: If you're unsure, contact the Nexus support team on Telegram (https://t.me/NexusOfficial) for assistance. They can help you confirm if your NXS coins need to be migrated before the fork.

Why do I need to migrate my legacy UTXO NXS before the hard fork?

If you do not migrate your legacy UTXO coins before the hard fork, you will lose access to those coins after the fork. The new protocol will no longer recognize the old UTXO format, so it’s critical to move your coins to the updated chain to avoid losing your funds.

How do I migrate my legacy UTXO coins to the new format?

To migrate your coins, you need to follow the official NXS migration guide provided by the Nexus team. This typically involves Downloading the Nexus Desktop wallet to convert your UTXO coins into the new Tritium format, ensuring they remain accessible after the hard fork.

What happens if I don’t migrate my UTXO coins before the hard fork?

If you fail to migrate your coins, they will effectively become obsolete and inaccessible once the hard fork occurs. You will not be able to use or transfer them after the fork date.

Is there a deadline for migrating my legacy coins?

Yes, there will be a specific deadline by which you must complete the migration of your UTXO coins. Make sure to check the official Nexus channels for the exact cutoff date, as any coins left un-migrated after the deadline will no longer be supported.

Is there a deadline for migrating my legacy coins?

No, once the hard fork happens, the legacy UTXO coins will no longer be supported, meaning you won’t be able to trade or transact them. You must migrate them beforehand to ensure they remain usable.

What should I do if I miss the migration deadline?

If you miss the migration deadline, your legacy UTXO coins will be lost. It’s crucial to act before the cutoff date. After the deadline, there will be no way to recover or access those funds.

Are there any risks associated with migrating my coins?

As with any blockchain migration, there may be minor risks involved, such as errors during the migration process. It is recommended to follow the official guidance and contact support if you run into any errors.

What resources are available to help me with the migration process?

Nexus will provide step-by-step instructions and FAQs on how to migrate your UTXO coins. You can also reach out to the support team via the official Nexus channels for assistance during the migration process.

Do I need to do anything after the hard fork If I’m already migrated?

Once the hard fork is completed, you will need to update your desktop or mobile wallet to the newest version that will be released after the hard fork. If you are staking, mining, or running a node, you will also need to update.
